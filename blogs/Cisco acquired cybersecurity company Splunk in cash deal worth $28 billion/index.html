Cisco acquired cybersecurity company Splunk in cash deal worth $28 billion

June 15,2023

Cisco has announced its acquisition of the cybersecurity software firm Splunk for $157 per share in a cash transaction valued at approximately $28 billion. This strategic move is part of Cisco's ongoing efforts to expand its cybersecurity offerings through acquisitions.

Splunk's stock experienced a significant 20% surge in premarket trading in response to the news, while Cisco's shares saw a 5% decrease.

Cisco's Chairman and CEO, Chuck Robbins, emphasized the importance of this acquisition in enhancing organizations' security and resilience. He stated, "From threat detection and response to threat prediction and prevention, we will help make organizations of all sizes more secure and resilient." The transaction is anticipated to be finalized in the third quarter of 2024.

Cisco foresees positive cash flow and improved gross margins within the first year after completing the acquisition, with an increase in Cisco's non-GAAP earnings per share expected by the second year. The financing of the deal will involve a combination of cash and debt.

Robbins also highlighted their ambition to become one of the world's largest software companies as a result of this collaboration.

Although the acquisition received mixed reactions from analysts, with concerns about potential product overlap, regulatory scrutiny, and the purchase price, both Robbins and Splunk CEO Gary Steele addressed these concerns. Steele pointed out that they still have a substantial customer base reliant on their customer-managed environment capabilities. Robbins also mentioned that they did not anticipate requiring regulatory approval for Splunk's operations in China.

Splunk specializes in cybersecurity by assisting enterprises in monitoring and analyzing their data to mitigate hacking risks and expedite technical issue resolution. Cisco, on the other hand, manufactures and sells telecommunications and networking equipment along with a suite of complementary software.

In case the deal falls through due to Cisco's withdrawal or regulatory intervention, Cisco will be liable to pay Splunk a termination fee of $1.48 billion, as outlined in a regulatory filing. If Splunk chooses to cancel the deal for any reason, they will be obligated to pay Cisco a breakup fee of $1 billion.

In 2023 alone, Cisco has been active in the acquisition market, having already acquired four companies. These include Armorblox, a platform focused on threat detection, Oort, specializing in identity management, and Valtix and Lightspin, both companies specializing in cloud security.

The legal advisory teams for this significant deal were Tidal Partners, Simpson Thacher, and Cravath, Swaine & Moore representing Cisco, while Qatalyst Partners, Morgan Stanley, and Skadden, Arps, Slate, Meagher & Flom advised Splunk.

This acquisition marks a pivotal moment in Cisco's ongoing expansion efforts in the cybersecurity sector. By incorporating Splunk's expertise and technology into its portfolio, Cisco aims to offer comprehensive security solutions that cater to a wide range of organizations, from threat detection and response to predictive and preventive security measures. The success of this venture will be closely monitored by industry experts and investors as it unfolds in the coming quarters.
